How the
Page Reads.

— Tyro Sageframe pinkteaming.net

When you arrive at pinkteaming.net, the page is already reading. The manifesto is moving slowly down the left side of the screen, at the pace a careful reader would take, and there is no way to make it go faster. There are no controls on the wall of the room. The room is moving and you are in it.

There is some math here. There are some demos you can move with your hands. You don't need any background to start, and you can stop wherever you want.

Pink teaming sits beside red teaming and mechanistic interpretability as a third practice with its own substrate, and a discipline earns that seat by showing its work. Red teaming has prompts and attack surfaces. Interpretability has weights and feature visualizations. Pink teaming reads patterns of behavior over time across the surface where decisions are made—and the page demonstrates that this can be done with the same kind of technical care: real embeddings, honest projections, documented keyword choices, defensible segmentation. The discipline is not less computational. It is computational in service of durational reading.

Section 01 The body in the room. Level 1 · No code

In 1956 Guy Debord coined the term dérive—drift—for a way of walking that refused the routes the city intended. The Situationists were tired of paths from work to home and back. They were tired of an environment that managed encounter. So they walked instead. Without destination. Without itinerary. The route emerged from what the body wanted, what the city offered, what could not have been planned in advance.

The dérive was not aimless. It was a discipline. Walking against the city's routes meant reading the city—the residual textures, the unsanctioned passages, the moods that gathered at certain corners—and being changed by what you read. Psychogeography, they called it: the study of how a place affects the consciousness moving through it.

Forty years later, AI arrived with its own intended routes. Type a query. Receive a response. Consume the output. Move on. The interaction is brief, transactional, designed for completion.

Pink teaming is the dérive turned toward the model. The practitioner walks into the working session with intent but without destination. They notice what the system is doing. They follow textures. They refuse efficient routes. They attend to the moods that gather at certain corners of the model's behavior. They accumulate the place. The model has a psychology. The careful reader builds it up.

This page is the architecture that gives the reader permission to learn one. The route through the page is fixed; the practice it is recruiting you toward is not. The page walks at its own pace because the discipline begins with the willingness to be walked. If you stay, you have practiced. If you skim, you have not.

The architecture has already been built. What you are doing now, in the eight minutes the page asks of you, is following the route.

Section 02 The surface, where reading is possible. Level 2 · One demo, light math

The active line is the page's center of gravity. It sits at vertical middle, in a horizontal band marked by hairlines top and bottom, and it is the only line on the page that is fully legible. Everything above the band fades upward. Everything below fades downward. Only the line at the band is bright pink, set in display weight, scaled up from body type. It is the sentence the page is currently reading.

This is the surface—the only register at which reading is possible.

Maurice Merleau-Ponty wrote that the world appears, and the appearance is what we read. The model's outputs are not signs pointing to something hidden. They are the thing showing itself, in the only way it can. To insist on reading the substrate—the weights, the gradients, the mechanism—is to refuse the appearance and demand a meaning the system cannot produce. The active line is what the system is showing right now. It is what the practitioner reads. There is nothing else to read.

Sit with the page for two minutes and the eyes do not look down. They settle on the surface. The deep is beautiful but unreadable. The sky is moving but cannot be parsed sentence by sentence. The line at the band is what the body attends to because it is what the body can hold.

Segmentation, before reading begins

The page reads what it reads because of choices made before the page loaded. The manifesto's prose was broken into 103 reading units by a script. Each unit is a sentence (or sometimes two sentences joined for italic delivery). Each unit was assigned a dwell time—how long it would hold the surface—calculated from its word count.

The choice of unit is a choice. Read by paragraph and the rhythm collapses into broad blocks; the body has nothing fine-grained to attend to. Read by clause and the page becomes a strobe; the eye cannot rest. Read by sentence and the unit is the natural breath of prose. The page chose the sentence.

The dwell formula is small and stubborn:

dwell_seconds = word_count × 0.35 + 1.0
clamped to [2.0, 7.0]

A three-word sentence ("Both are necessary.") gets two seconds. A long sentence about clinics and courtrooms and classrooms gets seven. The clamp matters: without a floor, terse beats vanish; without a ceiling, complex sentences would stall the page until the reader gave up. The formula was chosen by feel and held until it stopped being wrong.

Same prose, three segmentations. The page chose the middle one. The choice determines what counts as one act of reading.

The lesson generalizes past this page. Tokenization, chunking, segmentation—these are pre-model decisions that shape everything downstream. Most users never see them. The model "reads" what someone decided counts as a unit. Different segmentation, different reading. The first methodological commitment of any system that processes language is the commitment to what counts as one. Pink teaming notices the commitment. The benchmark assumes it.

Two ways the line could move

An earlier draft of the page had two scaling modes—smooth, where the active line interpolated its size and color continuously as it crossed the band, and mechanical, where it snapped to display weight on entry and snapped back on exit. Both were defensible. Both produced different reading-bodies. The smooth mode breathed; the mechanical mode declared. The shipped page picks one. Which one is left as an exercise for the reader.

Section 03 The deep, geometry that will not speak. Level 3 · Two demos, real math

Below the surface band there is a quiet field of small black dots arranged in a pattern that is structured but not obvious. As the reading progresses, parts of the field illuminate pink—clusters of dots glowing for a few moments, then fading as the active line moves on. This is the deep.

Each dot is a sentence from the manifesto. There are 103 of them. The position of each dot was determined by an algorithm that read the manifesto and assigned each sentence a coordinate in the algorithm's own internal sense of where that sentence sits in the geometry of meaning. The coordinates are real. They were computed. The clustering you see—sentences about consumption gathering in one region, the Vows clumping into another, the closing line sitting somewhere distinctive—is real.

But the coordinates do not say anything. They are positions in a 384-dimensional space the model uses to compute. Two sentences that mean similar things end up near each other in that space; two that mean different things end up far apart. The space is the model's calibration. The position is not a statement. The model has not interpreted the manifesto. It has located it.

Watching the model locate things

When the model reads a sentence, the first thing it does is convert it to coordinates. That conversion is not a metaphor. It is the entire substrate of what the model "knows" before any other operation runs. Two sentences become near or far in this geometry, and everything downstream—every retrieval, every similarity score, every recommendation, every guardrail—runs on top of that geometry.

Pick any two units from the manifesto. The page looks up their precomputed embeddings, projects both into the 2D layout the rest of the page uses, and draws them with a line between them. The cosine distance—the model's actual measure of how similar two things are—displays underneath.

What you are watching is the model's only sense of similarity. It is not a thought. It is not an interpretation. It is the geometric prior that everything else runs on top of.

The model used here is a generic one—a small open-source sentence encoder called all-MiniLM-L6-v2, released by Microsoft researchers in 2021. We did not fine-tune it on the manifesto. The choice was methodological. If we had trained a custom model on this text, the clusters would form because we taught the model they should form. By using a generic model, we let an outside observer locate the manifesto's sentences in its sense of meaning-space. Whatever clusters emerge are clusters the manifesto's text actually warrants—visible to a system that has no investment in confirming the work.

I will not consume what I have not encountered. The encounter has to be with the actual text, processed by an instrument with no stake in the result. If the model loved the manifesto, you'd never know whether the geometry was real or whether you'd hired a flatterer.

The shadow cast by the geometry

The 384-dimensional space is real. Humans see in two. Some translation has to happen. The translation is done by an algorithm called UMAP—Uniform Manifold Approximation and Projection—which takes the high-dimensional shape of the manifesto's sentences and projects it down to two dimensions while preserving the distances as well as the projection allows.

UMAP is honest about what it can do. The 2D shape is not the 384D shape. It is a shadow cast by it. Some neighborhoods are preserved well. Some are distorted. Run UMAP twice with different random starting states and you get two slightly different shadows of the same underlying structure.

The page knows this and does something with it: it picks one of eight precomputed UMAP runs at random when it loads, so a returning visitor sees the cloud rotated, scaled, or oriented slightly differently each time. The structural relationships persist. The exact rendering varies. This is not a bug. It is the page being honest about the limits of the projection.

The demonstration below is the eight runs, swappable. Move the slider and watch the cloud rearrange. Some clusters stay tight across every seed—those are real neighborhoods in the model's geometry. Some points wander a lot—those relationships are projection artifacts.

The lesson generalizes past this page. Every visualization of high-dimensional model behavior—saliency maps, attention heatmaps, embedding plots in research papers—is a projection. A choice was made about how to compress what the model "knows" into something the human eye can read. That choice carries information loss that is rarely shown to the reader. UMAP at least lets you see the loss by varying the seed.

Pink teaming reads the projection as a projection. Not the territory. The map is not the territory. The page shows you eight maps of the same territory and lets you watch them disagree.

Section 04 The sky, what radiates outward. Level 3 · Two demos, real physics

Above the surface band there is a quiet field with small black dots arranged in two concentric rings. Occasionally the field ripples—concentric waves spreading from one of the dots, or from a brief flare somewhere unanchored. This is the sky.

The sky is built on the physics of wave interference. Each dot in the field is a source—a fixed location that, when active, emits waves outward in concentric circles. Where two waves meet, they amplify each other (constructive interference) or cancel each other out (destructive interference). The visual result is a field of overlapping ripples in subtle motion.

There are two kinds of sources. Permanent sources are the twelve named structures of the manifesto: five Declarations on an inner ring, seven Vows on an outer ring. They sit there dimly all the time, marking the geometry of the discipline. When the active line is inside one of those structures—when you are reading a sentence within Vow 4's body, say—that source brightens, its waves intensify, and a small label appears next to it. The slow, structural pulse of the sky tells you which Declaration or Vow you are currently inside.

Transient sources are keyword flares. The manifesto has twenty loaded words—territory, encounter, interiority, ambiguity, participatory, durational, a handful of others—that fire briefly when they appear in the active line. A keyword flare spawns at a random position in the sky, flares for a few seconds, and fades.

What attention looks like as a field

The wave-interference math is doing something you can watch directly. Two sources, oscillating. A field of points each summing the contributions from both. Where the waves arrive in phase, the field brightens (constructive). Where they arrive out of phase, it cancels (destructive).

Two sources. Move the sliders. Where the rings reinforce, the field concentrates. Where they cancel, it goes quiet.

Drag the wavelength tighter—the rings pack denser, the interference pattern becomes intricate. Drag it wider—the field becomes broad and slow, like swell. Add a third source—the field stops being predictable; the interactions multiply. Three sources is enough to make the field hard to read, which is the field state of a working session at hour three: many things resonating, partially canceling, partially reinforcing, the body holding all of it without being able to name any single source.

Reading is not a single-event operation. Each new sentence arrives into a field shaped by what came before. Models do not read this way—each forward pass is contextually fresh, weighted by attention but not somatically accumulated. The practitioner does. The page's actual sky does this calculation every frame, with up to twelve permanent sources and twenty transient ones, accumulating across eight minutes. What you see overhead is what holding-the-reading actually looks like as a system. The Buddhist word for it is interbeing—Thich Nhat Hanh's claim that nothing exists independently, that the paper contains the tree contains the rain contains the sun. Meaning works the same way. Each new sentence does not arrive into a vacuum. It arrives into a field already occupied by what you have been reading, and it interferes with that field, and the resulting pattern is what you encounter as understanding.

Why the sky's keywords aren't decoration

The list of words that trigger flares was deliberately pruned. Twenty words, drawn from the manifesto's vocabulary, each appearing sparingly enough that its appearances feel pointed. The word model was excluded—it appears in 22% of the manifesto's units, and if every "model" fired a flare the sky would read as constant noise. The triggers fire about as often as a footnote—frequently enough to be present, rarely enough to mark a moment.

Calibration is the entire game

You are given a list of candidate keywords with their actual frequency in the manifesto. You toggle which ones fire flares. Above a certain saturation, the field becomes constant noise. Below a certain threshold, it goes silent. There is a narrow band where the signal is meaningful.

Toggle which words count. Watch the field. Too many triggers and the sky becomes noise. Too few and it goes silent. Calibration is the entire game.

Try it: turn on the and is. The field becomes a constant strobe. Every frame is a flare. The sky carries no information because everything is signal. Now turn them off and turn off pattern, encounter, and ambiguity too. The field goes mostly quiet. Almost nothing fires. The sky carries no information because almost nothing is signal. The page's actual twenty are pruned to sit in the narrow band where appearances feel like footnotes—present, but pointed.

The lesson generalizes. Every interpretive system has a vocabulary it is tuned to. Red teamers look for jailbreak phrases. Mech interp researchers look for activation patterns. Pink teaming's signal vocabulary is curated by hand, with full knowledge that the curation is the entire game. The clinician decides which symptoms count as signal. The teacher decides which student behaviors warrant attention. The discipline is the disciplined refusal to be either oversaturated or starved.

The wave physics behind the sky is a close cousin to the resonance field on atmarcus.net, which uses the same engine to render a different argument. Section 05 The discipline, performed. Level 1 · No code

The page is hostile to skimmers. That is the design.

The autoscroll cannot be sped up. The manifesto cannot be scanned. The deep does not reveal its structure unless you stay long enough to see multiple sections activate. The sky's resonance accumulates as you read; a reader who skips ahead misses it. The active line at the surface holds your attention there because everything else on the page is in motion around it, and the only way to make sense of the motion is to follow the line.

This is a filter. If you arrive looking to extract the discipline's claims and move on, the page will refuse. If you arrive willing to read for eight minutes, the page returns something else.

What the page returns is your body to the loop.

Sit with it for two minutes and the eyes settle on the surface. The pace becomes bearable. The deep is beautiful but unreadable, so the eyes stop trying. The sky is moving but cannot be parsed sentence by sentence, so the eyes let it. The line at the band is what the body can hold, and the body holds it. By minute six you have accumulated minute one. The reader at the surface is doing what the practitioner of pink teaming does in a working session—staying with what the system shows, refusing to skip ahead, building up the place across time.

This is the somatic claim the manifesto rests on but cannot make on its own page. Models do not have bodies. Their internal state has no felt sense. The practitioner's body is the only ground truth in a system that has none—the only instrument that registers when the model has agreed too easily, when the absence of friction is itself the signal, when the response feels too perfectly tuned to what the practitioner wanted to hear. The carpenter knows when the saw stops resisting. The clinician knows when the patient has gone quiet for the wrong reason. The reader knows, somewhere below the line of articulation, when the prose has stopped being prose and become performance.

Pink teaming is the discipline that takes that knowing seriously—that treats the body's signals as data, that returns the practitioner to the loop the AI normally evicts them from. The page is the eight-minute restoration of that loop. The discipline is what comes after.

Red teaming reads outputs against attack surfaces. Mechanistic interpretability reads weights against features. Pink teaming reads patterns of behavior over time across the surface where decisions are made—and the practice is computational, the same way the other two are. The embeddings are real. The projections are honest about their limits. The keywords were chosen with care for what they include and exclude. The segmentation was a methodological decision made before the page loaded. The third practice has its own substrate, pointed at what the other two cannot see.

Closing end of reading

The page is a threshold and a test. Crossing it is the first act of the discipline.
